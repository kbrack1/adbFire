"USB Drives/Samba" The Mount/Unmount buttons mount/unmount all connected USB drives or sticks. If checked, the "Mount drives at boot" checkbox in Device Setup mounts your devices at boot.

Mounting USB mass storage devices requires installation of busybox.

Mounted drives (and /sdcard) are exported to the network via Samba, a form of the SMB/CIFS networking protocol.

The Samba server you should see on your network is called adbFire. The storage is exported as sdcard and usb. Usb is a folder containing all your /dev/block/sd* devices. They ar mounted as drive1,drive2,drive3, etc in the order in which they are processed.

It's an unfortunate reality that assigning mount points by device name (/dev/sda1, /dev/sdb1) is unreliable. Now we have the ability to mount a drive to a specific location by its label or UUID, the unique id that is assigned when a device is formatted.

This release of adbFire has a script, /system/xbin/mntdrives2.sh, that you must edit to assign UUIDs or labels to specific mount points (e.g. /storage/usb/movies), but future releases will have functions that automate this process, making it point-and-click. If you're interested, read the script and post any questions you may have.

If you have questions or comments, please email jocala@jocala.com or visit one of the forums linked below. adbFire produces a log file, adbfire.log, with each run. Include it in your email or post if you're having problems.
