Swap /data
This option allows you to use external storage as your /data partition. This technique was originally ported to the Fire TV by jmandawg@xda, but was restricted to /dev/sda1, and no other devices could be attached.

adbFire's enhanced busybox adds the ability to mount drives by UUID, allowing you to mount any individual drive wherever you want, so now you can designate a /data drive and have as many other drives as you please attached for movies, music. animae or whatever.

First, adding a data drive is not particularly dangerous, but it's really a good idea to have ClockWork Mod Recovery installed. Since the Fire TV has a locked boot-loader and is currently unrootable, you should protect yourself.

You will need an powered external drive, a USB stick or Flash card attached of sufficient capacity to make this worth your while. It will need to be partitioned and formatted as EXT4. Windows users, you can use a http://www.sysresccd.org Linux Rescue Disk or download http://www.partitionwizard.com's Partition Wizard for free to prepare your storage device.

OS X users, if you don't have access to Fusion or Parallels for a Linux partition, Paragon has http://www.paragon-software.com has ExtFS for Mac, it's not free ($19.99) but does offer a 10 day fully working demo. And the Linux Rescue Disk is an option for most Macs as well.

Get your drive partitioned and plugged in. Most hard drives require a powered USB hub. Sticks and cards usually don't. Don't mount the external device yet. Once the device is plugged in, select Swap /data from the Root menu

A Drive selection dialog will pop up showing the external EXT4 USB devices attached to the Fire TV. Double-click your drive or click and press "Ok". Next, a dialog similar to this will appear:

---
Drive UUID="c67de5e7-481e-f8eb-4c4d-e7c390848468" selected.

Proceed with /data swap prep for this drive? This may be a lengthy process, depending
on the amount of material on your Fire TV /data partition.

A popup message will appear when prep is finished

Yes/No
---

Select Ok to proceed or Cancel to cancel the operation. As the dialog says, when you proceed it may be a lengthy process; it's copying up to almost 5Gb from the /data partition to the external device. It's not unusual for the length of time to max out the progress indicator. In testing, a 1Gb data partition takes almost 10 minutes to complete.

Once complete a popup will appear notifying you of the prep operation's success and offering to reboot. At this point your new /data partition is ready.

If you have questions or comments, please email jocala@jocala.com or visit one of the forums linked below. adbFire produces a log file, adbfire.log, with each run. Include it in your email or post if you're having problems.
